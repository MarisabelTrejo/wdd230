White Space

The New Yorker

newyorker.com

This page gives my eyes rest. I read the text and then I see how it relates to the picture, that's it. Notice the white space does not have to filled with any text or pictures. Instead, the white space that sorrounds the paragraph and pictures gives attention to the words and picture shown.

The rules of three

Apple

apple.com

If you were to put a 3x3 grid against this picture, you would notice how items on the screen would follow the pattern and be divided into each section. These spots capture the viewers attention. It gives the page a sense of balance.

The rule of Hierarchy

Candid Coco

eatcandid.com

This one is my favorite example. As we start at the top, we have a picture of their logo. Gradually the width of their description/picture becomes bigger like a pyramid. This takes the viewers eyes from the top all the way to the bottom.
